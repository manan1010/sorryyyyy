Dearest Riya,

I owe you this — not just words, but the right words.

You were in the middle of something important. You were trying to fix a problem for someone, doing what you always do — showing up, stepping in, solving things quietly. And me? I looked at a screenshot, saw you talking to someone else, and felt ignored. I let that feeling grow into something unfair.

What I didn't see in that moment was how much you always step forward for me. You call when you're on duty. You call while you're on your bike. You reach out first, every single time. You understand me in ways most people never bother to try.

And when I reacted the way I did — you didn't argue. You didn't beg. You just explained everything, calmly, clearly, because that's who you are. No drama. No games. Just pure, honest Riya.

That hurt me more than any fight could — realizing that you'd handled it with so much grace, and I hadn't given you even a fraction of that trust back.

And then — even in a bad mood that night — you said goodbye to me like this:
"Good night ✨ Sweet dreams 😘 See you soon 💕 Tata ✨"

That's not just a friend, Riya. That's something rare. And I almost didn't see it.

I'm sorry I didn't trust you. I'm sorry I made you feel like with you, things always go sideways. You deserve someone who sees your heart first — and I wasn't that, in that moment.

But I want to be. Starting now. 🤗

— Always yours, bestie 🤍
